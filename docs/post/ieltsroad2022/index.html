Road to IELTS

Mar 13, 2022 · 6 min read · IELTS Learning  ·
Share on:

"I'm going to take IELTS in about one month. This blog is the record of how I try to improve in English, especially in writing and speaking."

Foreword

Hi there! Don't know how you stumbled upon this blog. Honestly, this blog is just a place for me to record my progress in improving my writing and speaking skills. I'll mark some of the websites and Youtube channels that I find useful during my learning journey, but don't expect to learn much. I'll keep updating till I finally get my IELTS result, so stay tuned if you are interested!

My current level

In 2020, I had my last IELTS exam. I would say that I got pretty lucky, as I got a 9 in reading and an 8 in listening, which saved me from my below par score in writing (6.0) and speaking (6.5), resulting in a total score of 7.5.

After 2 years, i.e. today, I've registered for another IELTS exam. This time, my aim is to get at least 7.0 in all subjects and total score as 7.5. It is never an easy challenge, especially I've got only one and a half month to start working on my English skills.

Things I'll do

Reading & Listening

For reading and listening, all I will do is some IELTS exercise that I rent from the library. As my time is precious, I believe that I should put my focus on getting better in writing and speaking.

Writing & Speaking

For now, I've identified some of my weakness in writing and speaking.

Writing Tree

First, my biggest weakness: grammar. I feel like that I never really think throuoghly on why am I using certain tense, but just using it by will. Also, my sentence structure is bad that I never write good sentences. It's okay for daily conversation, but certainly bad for exams like IELTS. Therefore, I need to find way to learn how to use tenses appropriately and write better structured sentence.

For now, I'll study through a Youtube channel's video: Ms. Charlotte. I've gone through some of her videos, and I feel like that as a Hongkonger, its the first time I really listen to some good explaination in English grammar and sentence structure in Cantonese. (Sorry for any foreign viewer, if any (・∀・) )

Another weakness for me is IELTS writing structure. Last time, I had prepared nothing for the IELTS test, and therefore faced difficulties in writing articles that matched IELTS requirement. So therefore, I believe that pay more attention to the related things.

One website that I find very useful is IELTS Liz. Not only it gives a lot of advice on how to write an IELTS-format article, it also includes sample questions and answers that allow us to try writing. I believe that doing something is the best way to learn, so able to actually write something then have a model answer to reference on seems good for me.

My last weakness is vocabulary, which I guess you should have noticed it when you are reading this and my other blog. I find it hard to improve on, expecially when I usaully just use the first word that pops up in my brain.

IELTS Liz can help, but I need more than that to really memorize them and utilize the new words in my article and conversation. What I can think of is to use Anki, a software that I've mentioned before (My Blog 'Learning how to learn') Through this software, hopefully I can memorize more words and therefore use a variety of them!

My plan

Currently, my aim is to at least write an article thrice a week, i.e. 1 article in around 2-3 days. Besides that, I'll listen to videos, drop notes and add new words in my Anki. I'll update this in next week, so lets see how it goes!
